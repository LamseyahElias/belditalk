## Conversation 1: At the Café

**Setting:** You sit down at a café in the medina. The waiter approaches.

> **Waiter:** Salam! Merhba. Shno bghiti tshreb?
> *(Hello! Welcome. What would you like to drink?)*

> **You:** Salam! Bghit wahed l'qhwa nus nus, 3afak.
> *(Hello! I'd like a half-half coffee, please.)*

> **Waiter:** Nus nus, wakha. Shi haja khra?
> *(Half-half, okay. Anything else?)*

> **You:** Iyeh, wahed l'ma, 3afak.
> *(Yes, a water, please.)*

> **Waiter:** Wakha, daba njibha lik.
> *(Okay, I'll bring it to you now.)*

*— 10 minutes later —*

> **You:** 3afak, l'hsab.
> *(Please, the bill.)*

> **Waiter:** 25 dirham.
> *(25 dirhams.)*

> **You:** Tfddal. Shukran bzzaf.
> *(Here you go. Thank you very much.)*

> **Waiter:** Lah ybarek fik. Bslama!
> *(God bless you. Goodbye!)*

### Key Phrases
| Darija | English |
|--------|---------|
| Shno bghiti tshreb? | What do you want to drink? |
| Nus nus | Half-half (coffee with milk) |
| Shi haja khra? | Anything else? |
| Daba njibha lik | I'll bring it to you now |
| L'hsab | The bill |
| Tfddal | Here you go / Please (offering) |

### Cultural Note
Moroccan cafés are social hubs. Men often sit for hours watching football, chatting, or people-watching. Ordering just one coffee and sitting for 2 hours is completely normal and not rude. "Nus nus" (half espresso, half steamed milk) is the most popular café drink.

## Conversation 2: In a Taxi

**Setting:** You need to get from Jemaa el-Fna to Gueliz in Marrakech.

> **You:** Salam khouya. Tmshi li l'Gueliz?
> *(Hello brother. Will you take me to Gueliz?)*

> **Driver:** Gueliz? Iyeh. Rkeb.
> *(Gueliz? Yes. Get in.)*

> **You:** Bshhal mn hna l'Gueliz?
> *(How much from here to Gueliz?)*

> **Driver:** 30 dirham.
> *(30 dirhams.)*

> **You:** Bzzaf! 15 dirham.
> *(Too much! 15 dirhams.)*

> **Driver:** La, 25.
> *(No, 25.)*

> **You:** Wakha, 20. Safi?
> *(Okay, 20. Deal?)*

> **Driver:** Yallah, 20. Rkeb.
> *(Let's go, 20. Get in.)*

*— During the ride —*

> **You:** 3afak, habs hna 3la limen.
> *(Please, stop here on the right.)*

> **Driver:** Hna?
> *(Here?)*

> **You:** Iyeh, hna mzyan. Shukran khouya. Tfddal.
> *(Yes, here is good. Thanks brother. Here you go.)*

> **Driver:** Lah y3tik s'hha. Bslama!
> *(God give you health. Goodbye!)*

### Key Phrases
| Darija | English |
|--------|---------|
| Tmshi li...? | Will you take me to...? |
| Rkeb | Get in |
| Bshhal mn hna l...? | How much from here to...? |
| Habs hna | Stop here |
| 3la limen / 3la lisar | On the right / On the left |
| Lah y3tik s'hha | God give you health (common thanks) |

### Cultural Note
Always agree on the price BEFORE getting in. Petit taxis (small colored cars) should use meters in cities, but many don't. Negotiate firmly but friendly. Calling the driver "khouya" (brother) builds rapport. Round up a dirham or two as a small tip.

## Conversation 3: At the Souk

**Setting:** You're at the souk looking at leather bags.

> **Seller:** Salam! Merhba! Dkhol, dkhol. Shuf ghir shuf.
> *(Hello! Welcome! Come in, come in. Just look, just look.)*

> **You:** Salam. Had sh-shanta zwina. Bshhal?
> *(Hello. This bag is beautiful. How much?)*

> **Seller:** Hadi? Jild hqiqi. 800 dirham.
> *(This one? Real leather. 800 dirhams.)*

> **You:** 800?! Ghali bzzaf khouya!
> *(800?! Way too expensive brother!)*

> **Seller:** Jild hqiqi, khidma d'lyed. Shhal t3ti?
> *(Real leather, handmade. How much will you give?)*

> **You:** N3tik 300.
> *(I'll give you 300.)*

> **Seller:** 300? La la la. Rah khlsatni bzzaf. 600, akhir taman.
> *(300? No no no. That costs me a lot. 600, final price.)*

> **You:** Ma 3ndish 600. 400, safi.
> *(I don't have 600. 400, that's it.)*

> **Seller:** Aji, aji. Rak sahbi dyali. 450 w safi.
> *(Come, come. You're my friend. 450 and done.)*

> **You:** Wakha. 450. 3tini iyaha.
> *(Okay. 450. Give it to me.)*

> **Seller:** Tbark Allah 3lik. Rani n'lffha lik.
> *(Bless you. I'll wrap it for you.)*

### Key Phrases
| Darija | English |
|--------|---------|
| Dkhol | Come in |
| Shuf ghir shuf | Just look |
| Jild hqiqi | Real leather |
| Khidma d'lyed | Handmade |
| Shhal t3ti? | How much will you give? |
| N3tik... | I'll give you... |
| Akhir taman | Final price |
| 3tini iyaha | Give it to me |

### Cultural Note
Bargaining is expected and enjoyed. Start at about 40% of their first price. Walk away if they won't budge — they'll often call you back. Never bargain aggressively or disrespectfully. Smile, use "khouya/khti," and treat it as a friendly game. In fixed-price shops (supermarkets, chain stores), don't bargain.

## Conversation 4: Visiting a Moroccan Home

**Setting:** You're invited to dinner at a Moroccan friend's home.

> **Host (Mama):** Ahlan w sahlan! Dkhol, dkhol! Merhba bik!
> *(Welcome! Come in, come in! Welcome to you!)*

> **You:** Shukran khti. Tbark Allah 3la d'dar, zwina bzzaf.
> *(Thank you sister. Bless this house, it's very beautiful.)*

> **Host:** Lah ybarek fik. Gles, gles. Rah ndiru lik atay.
> *(God bless you. Sit, sit. We'll make you tea.)*

> **You:** Shukran. Mma kayn ghir riht bnina mn l'kuzina.
> *(Thank you. There's a delicious smell from the kitchen.)*

> **Host:** Derna lik tajine d'djaj. Nmshi njibha.
> *(We made you chicken tajine. I'll go get it.)*

*— Food is served —*

> **Host:** Bismillah. Kul, kul!
> *(In God's name. Eat, eat!)*

> **You:** Bismillah. Mmm, bnina bzzaf! Tbark Allah 3la ydik.
> *(In God's name. Mmm, very delicious! Bless your hands.)*

> **Host:** S'hha w raha. Zid, zid! Mazal bzzaf.
> *(Health and comfort. More, more! There's still a lot.)*

> **You:** Shbe3t, shukran. Kanet wakha bnina.
> *(I'm full, thank you. It was really delicious.)*

> **Host:** Lah y3tik s'hha. Dima merhba bik 3ndna.
> *(God give you health. You're always welcome at our home.)*

> **You:** Lah ykhlikom. Shukran 3la kul shi.
> *(God keep you. Thank you for everything.)*

### Key Phrases
| Darija | English |
|--------|---------|
| Ahlan w sahlan | Welcome |
| Tbark Allah 3la d'dar | Bless this house |
| Gles | Sit down |
| Bismillah | In God's name (said before eating) |
| Tbark Allah 3la ydik | Bless your hands (complimenting cooking) |
| S'hha w raha | Health and comfort (said to someone eating) |
| Shbe3t | I'm full |
| Dima merhba bik | You're always welcome |

### Cultural Note
When visiting a Moroccan home: remove shoes at the door, accept at least 3 glasses of mint tea (refusing the first is considered rude), compliment the food with "Tbark Allah 3la ydik," and eat with your RIGHT hand if eating from a shared dish. Bring a small gift (pastries, fruit, sugar). Say "Bismillah" before eating and "Hamdullah" after.

## Conversation 5: Making Friends

**Setting:** You meet someone at a language exchange event.

> **Them:** Salam! Rak bikhir?
> *(Hello! Are you good?)*

> **You:** Salam! Iyeh, hamdullah. W nta?
> *(Hello! Yes, thank God. And you?)*

> **Them:** Hamdullah. Smitk?
> *(Thank God. What's your name?)*

> **You:** Smiti [name]. W nta, shno smitk?
> *(My name is [name]. And you, what's your name?)*

> **Them:** Ana Youssef. Mnin nta?
> *(I'm Youssef. Where are you from?)*

> **You:** Ana mn [country]. Jit l'Maghrib bash nt3llem Darija.
> *(I'm from [country]. I came to Morocco to learn Darija.)*

> **Them:** Waaa, hadshi mzyan bzzaf! Shhal hadi w nta hna?
> *(Wow, that's really great! How long have you been here?)*

> **You:** 3ndi juj shhor hna. Kanb'ghi l'Maghrib bzzaf.
> *(I've been here two months. I love Morocco a lot.)*

> **Them:** W Darija dyalk mezyana! Wash bghiti nmshiw nshurbu shi qhwa?
> *(And your Darija is good! Do you want to go get a coffee?)*

> **You:** Iyeh, b'kul surur! Yallah nmshiw.
> *(Yes, with pleasure! Let's go.)*

> **Them:** Yallah! Rak sahbi daba.
> *(Let's go! You're my friend now.)*

### Key Phrases
| Darija | English |
|--------|---------|
| Smitk? | What's your name? |
| Mnin nta/nti? | Where are you from? |
| Jit l'Maghrib | I came to Morocco |
| Shhal hadi w nta hna? | How long have you been here? |
| Kanb'ghi | I love (ongoing) |
| Wash bghiti...? | Do you want to...? |
| B'kul surur | With pleasure |
| Rak sahbi daba | You're my friend now |

### Cultural Note
Moroccans are incredibly warm and hospitable. Don't be surprised if someone you just met invites you for coffee or even dinner at their home. Friendships form quickly and are genuine. Use "sahbi" (my friend, masc.) or "sahbti" (my friend, fem.) — it's a big compliment.
