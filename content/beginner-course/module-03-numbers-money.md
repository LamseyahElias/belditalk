# Module 3: Numbers & Money

## Overview
Numbers are everywhere — prices, phone numbers, addresses, ages, time. In this module, you'll master counting in Darija and learn to handle money like a local. Morocco uses Dirhams (MAD), but the old "riyal" counting system still confuses many visitors. We'll decode all of it.

## The Riyal System — IMPORTANT!

This confuses EVERY visitor. Here's the deal:

Morocco's official currency is the **Dirham (MAD)**. But many Moroccans — especially in markets, taxis, and rural areas — still quote prices in **riyals**, an old currency unit.

**The conversion: 1 Dirham = 20 Riyals**

So when someone says:
- "Mya riyal" (100 riyals) → they mean **5 dirhams**
- "Miyatayn riyal" (200 riyals) → they mean **10 dirhams**
- "Alf riyal" (1000 riyals) → they mean **50 dirhams**

**How to handle it:** If you're unsure, just ask: "B dirham?" (In dirhams?) or "Shhal b dirham?" (How much in dirhams?)

## Dialogue 1: At the Market

> **You:** Salam! Bshhal had l'brtqal? (How much are these oranges?)
> **Vendor:** Tlatin dirham l'kilo. (30 dirhams per kilo)
> **You:** Ghali shwiya! 3shrin dirham? (A bit expensive! 20 dirhams?)
> **Vendor:** Khmsa w 3shrin, akhir taman. (25, final price)
> **You:** Wakha. 3tini jooj kilo, 3afak. (OK. Give me 2 kilos, please)

**Translation:**
> **You:** Hello! How much are these oranges?
> **Vendor:** 30 dirhams per kilo.
> **You:** A bit expensive! 20 dirhams?
> **Vendor:** 25, final price.
> **You:** OK. Give me 2 kilos, please.

---

## Dialogue 2: Paying at a Restaurant

> **You:** 3afak, bshhal l'addition? (Please, how much is the bill?)
> **Waiter:** Miya w khmsin dirham. (150 dirhams)
> **You:** Tfddal. (Here you go) *hands money*
> **Waiter:** Ma 3ndish srf. 3ndk shwiya d srf? (I don't have change. Do you have some change?)
> **You:** 3ndi miya dirham w khmsin dirham. Hak. (I have 100 and 50. Here.)
> **Waiter:** Shukran!

## Dialogue 4: Phone Number Exchange

> **You:** 3afak, ash howa numero dyalek? (Please, what's your number?)
> **Moroccan:** Zero stta, wahed jooj, tlata rb3a, khmsa stta, sb3a tmnya. 
> **You:** Smeh li, 3awed 3afak? (Sorry, repeat please?)
> **Moroccan:** 0-6-1-2-3-4-5-6-7-8.

Note: Moroccan phone numbers start with 06 or 07 and have 10 digits.

## Cultural Notes

### 1. The Riyal Trap
Some dishonest vendors in tourist areas will quote a price in riyals, hoping you'll pay that amount in dirhams (20x more!). If a price sounds weirdly high, ask: "B dirham wlla b riyal?" (In dirhams or riyals?)

### 2. Cash Is King
Morocco is still largely a cash economy, especially in souks, small shops, and taxis. Always carry small bills (20, 50, 100 dirham notes). Breaking a 200 MAD note at a small shop is annoying for everyone.

### 3. Tipping Culture
- Cafés: Round up or leave 2-5 MAD
- Restaurants: 10-15% for good service
- Parking attendants: 2-5 MAD
- Someone who helped with directions: 5-10 MAD (optional but appreciated)

### 4. Don't Flash Money
Count and handle money discreetly. Pulling out a thick wallet in a crowded souk is poor form and attracts attention.

### 5. Bargaining Expected
In souks and with taxis (without meter), bargaining is expected and even enjoyed. The first price is NEVER the real price. More on this in Module 6.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Not Clarifying Dirham vs. Riyal
**Wrong:** Hearing "mya" and assuming 100 dirhams.
**Right:** Ask "B dirham?" to confirm. It could be 100 riyals (= 5 dirhams).

### ❌ Mistake 2: Using MSA Numbers
**Wrong:** Wahid, ithnan, thalatha (Standard Arabic)
**Right:** Wahed, jooj, tlata (Darija)
Darija numbers, especially 2 (jooj) and 3 (tlata), are completely different from MSA.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Wrong Number Order
**Wrong:** "3shrin w khmsa" (twenty and five) for 25
**Right:** "Khmsa w 3shrin" (five and twenty)
In Darija, the unit comes BEFORE the ten — just like German!

### ❌ Mistake 4: Not Carrying Small Change
**Wrong:** Trying to pay a 10 DH taxi ride with a 200 DH note.
**Right:** Always have 10s, 20s, and 50s ready. ATMs often give 200 DH notes — break them at a supermarket first.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Being Afraid to Ask the Price
**Wrong:** Being too shy to ask and just paying whatever.
**Right:** Always ask "Bshhal?" — even locals do it. There's no shame in knowing the price.
