# Module 5: Getting Around — Directions & Transportation

## Overview
Morocco's cities are vibrant, winding, and sometimes wonderfully confusing. Whether you're navigating the labyrinth of a medina, catching a petit taxi across town, or figuring out which bus goes to the beach, this module gives you the Darija you need to get where you're going — and ask for help when you're lost.

## Dialogue 1: Finding a Place on Foot

> **You:** Smeh li, fin kayna Jama3 Lfna?
> **Local:** Sir nichan, w men b3d dour l-lisar.
> **You:** Wash b3id?
> **Local:** La, qrib. Ghir 3shr dqayq mashi.
> **You:** Shukran bezzaf!
> **Local:** Bla jmil!

**Translation:**
> **You:** Excuse me, where is Jemaa el-Fna?
> **Local:** Go straight, and then turn left.
> **You:** Is it far?
> **Local:** No, it's close. Just 10 minutes walking.
> **You:** Thank you very much!
> **Local:** You're welcome!

## Dialogue 2: Taking a Petit Taxi

> **You:** Salam, l-mahatta 3afak.
> **Driver:** L-mahatta dyal tobis wla tran?
> **You:** Dyal tran, 3afak. Bshhal?
> **Driver:** 15 dirham.
> **You:** Wakha, yallah.
> *(arriving)*
> **You:** Wqef hna, 3afak. Shukran!
> **Driver:** Bslama!

**Translation:**
> **You:** Hello, to the station please.
> **Driver:** The bus station or train station?
> **You:** Train station, please. How much?
> **Driver:** 15 dirhams.
> **You:** Okay, let's go.
> *(arriving)*
> **You:** Stop here, please. Thank you!
> **Driver:** Goodbye!

## Dialogue 3: Lost in the Medina

> **You:** Smeh li, ana tleft. Fin bab l-mdina?
> **Local:** Bab l-mdina? Dour l-ymen hna, w sir nichan hta l-zenqa lkbira.
> **You:** L-zenqa lkbira... w men b3d?
> **Local:** Men b3d dour l-lisar, ghadi tchouf l-bab nichan.
> **You:** Wash b3id bezzaf?
> **Local:** La, qrib, ghir khams dqayq.
> **You:** Tbark Allah 3lik, shukran!

**Translation:**
> **You:** Excuse me, I'm lost. Where is the city gate?
> **Local:** The city gate? Turn right here, and go straight until the big street.
> **You:** The big street... and then?
> **Local:** Then turn left, you'll see the gate straight ahead.
> **You:** Is it very far?
> **Local:** No, close, just five minutes.
> **You:** God bless you, thank you!

## Dialogue 4: Taking a Grand Taxi

> **You:** Salam, wash kayn taxi l-Chefchaouen?
> **Taxi Stand Guy:** Iyeh, hak, hada ghadi l-Chefchaouen. Bqa blan wahed.
> **You:** Bshhal?
> **Taxi Stand Guy:** 75 dirham l-wahed.
> **You:** Wash sir nichan wla kayn arrêts?
> **Taxi Stand Guy:** Nichan, 3 swaye3.
> **You:** Wakha, yallah.

**Translation:**
> **You:** Hello, is there a taxi to Chefchaouen?
> **Taxi Stand Guy:** Yes, here, this one is going to Chefchaouen. One seat left.
> **You:** How much?
> **Taxi Stand Guy:** 75 dirhams per person.
> **You:** Does it go straight or are there stops?
> **Taxi Stand Guy:** Straight, 3 hours.
> **You:** Okay, let's go.

## Cultural Notes

### 🚕 Petit Taxi vs. Grand Taxi
Morocco has two completely different taxi systems:
- **Petit taxis** operate within cities. They're small cars (often Fiat Unos or Dacia Logans), color-coded by city (red in Casablanca, blue in Rabat, beige in Fez). They use a meter — **always insist on "l-compteur" (the meter)**.
- **Grand taxis** are shared taxis that travel between cities. They're usually older Mercedes sedans that seat 6 passengers (3 in back, 3 in front). They leave when full. You pay per seat.

### 🗺️ Moroccans Give Landmarks, Not Street Names
When asking for directions, don't expect "turn on Avenue Mohammed V." Moroccans navigate by landmarks: "Go past the mosque," "Next to the hammam," "Behind the big market." Learn to listen for place references.

### 🚶 Walking Culture
Moroccans walk a lot, especially in medinas where cars can't go. "Qrib" (close) is relative — it could mean 5 minutes or 20 minutes depending on who you ask. When someone says "qrib," ask "bshhal dyal dqayq?" (how many minutes?).

### 📱 Modern Getting Around
In big cities like Casablanca, Rabat, and Marrakech, ride-hailing apps like **InDrive** and **Careem** are widely used. You can avoid price negotiations entirely. The Casablanca and Rabat tramways are modern, clean, and efficient.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Confusing Ymen and Lisar
Left and right are easy to mix up in any language. Practice by walking around and saying "ymen" every time you turn right, "lisar" for left, until it's automatic.

### ❌ Not Insisting on the Meter
In petit taxis, always say "Dir l-compteur, 3afak" (Put on the meter, please). Some drivers will quote inflated prices to tourists. If they refuse the meter, get out and find another taxi.

### ❌ Saying "B3id" When You Mean "B3da"
"B3id" means far (adjective). "B3da" means "later" or "after." Don't mix them up. "Men b3d" means "after that" in giving directions.

### ❌ Expecting Exact Distances
Moroccans rarely give exact distances. You'll hear "qrib" (close) or "b3id shwiya" (a little far). Always ask "bshhal dyal dqayq?" (how many minutes?) for a clearer answer.

### ❌ Using "Nichan" Only for Directions
"Nichan" means straight, but it's also used to mean "exactly" or "right there." "Howa nichan temma" means "It's right there." Context matters!
