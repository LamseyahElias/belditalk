# Module 4: Food & Drinks

## Overview
Moroccan food is legendary — and knowing how to order, express preferences, and talk about food in Darija will transform your dining experience. From ordering mint tea in a café to navigating a food stall in Jemaa el-Fna, this module has you covered.

## Dialogue 1: Ordering at a Café

> **Waiter:** Salam! Ash bghiti? (Hello! What would you like?)
> **You:** Salam! Bghit wahed atay, 3afak. (Hello! I'd like one mint tea, please.)
> **Waiter:** B n3na3 wlla bla? (With mint or without?)
> **You:** B n3na3, w bla sokkar. (With mint, and without sugar.)
> **Waiter:** Shi haja khra? (Anything else?)
> **You:** W wahed qhwa noss noss. (And one half-half coffee.)
> **Waiter:** Wakha, tfdl. (OK, coming right up.)

**Translation Notes:**
- "Noss noss" = "half half" = half coffee, half milk (the most popular coffee order in Morocco)
- "B" = with, "Bla" = without
- "Shi haja khra" = something else / anything else

## Dialogue 2: At a Restaurant

> **You:** 3afak, ash 3ndkum lyoum? (Please, what do you have today?)
> **Waiter:** 3ndna tajine b djaj, tajine b lhem, w kuskus.
> **You:** Bghit tajine b djaj, 3afak.
> **Waiter:** W lshrab? (And to drink?)
> **You:** Wahed lma, 3afak.
> *(food arrives)*
> **Waiter:** Bssa7a w raha! (Bon appétit!)
> **You:** Allah y3tik ssa7a! (Thank you — may God give you health!)
> *(after eating)*
> **You:** Bnin bzzaf! Shukran! (Very delicious! Thank you!)

## Dialogue 3: Street Food

> **You:** Salam khouya! Bshhal had s'sandwich? (Hey brother! How much is this sandwich?)
> **Vendor:** 3shrin dirham. (20 dirhams)
> **You:** 3tini wahed b lhem w khodra. (Give me one with meat and vegetables.)
> **Vendor:** Harr wlla bla? (Spicy or not?)
> **You:** Shwiya d l'harr. (A little spicy.)
> **Vendor:** Tfddal! (Here you go!)
> **You:** Shukran! Bssa7a!

## Dialogue 4: Home Dinner Invitation

> **Host:** Tfddal, kul! Ma tkhmmsh! (Come on, eat! Don't be shy!)
> **You:** Shukran, bnin bzzaf had t'tajine! (Thank you, this tajine is very delicious!)
> **Host:** Kul ktar! (Eat more!)
> **You:** Shb3t, shukran. Kont bnin bzzaf. (I'm full, thank you. It was very delicious.)
> **Host:** Bghiti atay? (Want tea?)
> **You:** Iyeh, 3afak! (Yes, please!)

## Cultural Notes

### 1. Bread Is Sacred
In Morocco, bread (khobz) is treated with deep respect. Never throw bread in the trash. If you see bread on the ground, Moroccans will pick it up and place it somewhere elevated. Bread is at EVERY meal — it's your spoon, your fork, your plate.

### 2. Eating with Your Right Hand
Traditional Moroccan meals are eaten with the right hand, using bread to scoop food from a shared plate (tajine). Eat from the section directly in front of you — don't reach across to the other side.

### 3. The Tea Ritual
Mint tea ("atay b n3na3") is Morocco's social glue. Refusing tea is considered somewhat rude — accept at least one glass. It's traditionally poured from a height to create a frothy top. The host pours, NEVER pour yourself.

### 4. "Bssa7a w Raha"
This phrase (literally "with health and rest") is Morocco's "bon appétit." When someone says it to you, respond with "Allah y3tik ssa7a" (May God give you health). You'll hear this constantly.

### 5. Friday Couscous
Couscous on Friday is a sacred Moroccan tradition. Families gather for a big couscous lunch after Friday prayers. If you're invited to Friday couscous, you're being honored. Accept enthusiastically.

### 6. Moroccan Hospitality — They'll Overfeed You
Moroccans will INSIST you eat more. "Kul! Kul!" (Eat! Eat!) will be repeated constantly. They may physically put more food on your plate. It's love expressed through food. Say "Shb3t, shukran" (I'm full, thank you) and they'll still try. Smile and hold firm — or eat more!

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Ordering "Chai"
**Wrong:** "Bghit wahed chai" (that's South Asian/Turkish)
**Right:** "Bghit wahed atay"
In Morocco, tea is "atay" — always.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Pouring Your Own Tea
**Wrong:** Picking up the teapot and pouring for yourself.
**Right:** Wait for the host to pour. If you want more, hold up your glass or ask politely.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Eating with Your Left Hand
**Wrong:** Using your left hand to eat from a shared plate.
**Right:** Always use your right hand. If you're left-handed, make an effort — or use utensils if available.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Refusing Food Too Quickly
**Wrong:** "La, shukran" immediately when offered food.
**Right:** Accept at least a little. If you truly can't eat, say "Shb3t bzzaf, shukran, kont bnin" (I'm very full, thank you, it was delicious). Refusing food can seem like rejection of hospitality.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Not Complimenting the Food
**Wrong:** Eating silently and leaving.
**Right:** Say "Bnin bzzaf!" (Very delicious!) at least once. It means the world to the cook.
