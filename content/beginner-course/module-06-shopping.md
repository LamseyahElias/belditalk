# Module 6: Shopping & Bargaining — The Art of the Souk

## Overview
Shopping in Morocco isn't just buying things — it's a social ritual, a test of wit, and genuinely fun once you know how it works. Whether you're haggling for a leather bag in the Fez medina or buying spices in Marrakech, these phrases will help you navigate prices, negotiate like a local, and walk away with great deals and a smile.

## Dialogue 1: First Time at the Souk

> **You:** Salam! Bshhal hada?
> **Vendor:** Hada? 300 dirham, khoya. Jild mezyan, handmade.
> **You:** 300?! Ghali bezzaf!
> **Vendor:** Ghali? Hada jdid, qualité! 280 l-akher.
> **You:** La, bezzaf. 150.
> **Vendor:** 150?! La la la. 250.
> **You:** 180, akhir taman.
> **Vendor:** 200, w safi.
> **You:** Wakha, 200. Atini yah.
> **Vendor:** Tfddal, shukran khoya!

**Translation:**
> **You:** Hello! How much is this?
> **Vendor:** This? 300 dirhams, brother. Good leather, handmade.
> **You:** 300?! Way too expensive!
> **Vendor:** Expensive? This is new, quality! 280, final.
> **You:** No, too much. 150.
> **Vendor:** 150?! No no no. 250.
> **You:** 180, final price.
> **Vendor:** 200, and done.
> **You:** Okay, 200. Give it to me.
> **Vendor:** Here you go, thank you brother!

## Dialogue 2: Buying Spices

> **You:** Salam! Bshhal kilo dyal l-kamoun?
> **Spice Seller:** Salam! L-kamoun, 80 dirham l-kilo. Hada mezyan bezzaf, pur.
> **You:** W l-kharqoum?
> **Spice Seller:** L-kharqoum 120. Bghiti tshemm? (Want to smell?)
> **You:** Iyeh... mmm, 3jbni. Atini nos kilo dyal l-kamoun w rbe3 kilo dyal l-kharqoum.
> **Spice Seller:** Wakha. Hak, 70 dirham l-kol.
> **You:** Shukran! Fin nkhlas?
> **Spice Seller:** Hna, 3afak. Bslama!

**Translation:**
> **You:** Hello! How much is a kilo of cumin?
> **Spice Seller:** Hello! Cumin, 80 dirhams per kilo. This is very good, pure.
> **You:** And the turmeric?
> **Spice Seller:** Turmeric is 120. Want to smell?
> **You:** Yes... mmm, I like it. Give me half a kilo of cumin and a quarter kilo of turmeric.
> **Spice Seller:** Okay. Here, 70 dirhams total.
> **You:** Thank you! Where do I pay?
> **Spice Seller:** Right here, please. Goodbye!

## Dialogue 3: Walking Away (The Power Move)

> **Vendor:** Hada 400 dirham, khti. Pur argan.
> **You:** Ghali bezzaf. Ma 3ndish 400.
> **Vendor:** Goul li, bshhal bghiti t3ti?
> **You:** 200.
> **Vendor:** La, 350.
> **You:** 200, akhir haja. Ma bghitsh ktar.
> **Vendor:** 300, akhir taman dyali.
> **You:** Safi, shukran. Bslama.
> *(You start walking away)*
> **Vendor:** Aji aji! Wakha, 220 w safi!
> **You:** 200.
> **Vendor:** Yallah, 200. Hzha.

**Translation:**
> **You:** *(Vendor says)* This is 400 dirhams, sister. Pure argan.
> **You:** Way too expensive. I don't have 400.
> **Vendor:** Tell me, how much do you want to give?
> **You:** 200.
> **Vendor:** No, 350.
> **You:** 200, final offer. I don't want more.
> **Vendor:** 300, my final price.
> **You:** Okay then, thank you. Goodbye.
> *(You start walking away)*
> **Vendor:** Come come! Okay, 220 and done!
> **You:** 200.
> **Vendor:** Fine, 200. Take it.

## Dialogue 4: At a Regular Shop (No Bargaining)

> **You:** Salam, 3ndkom may (water)?
> **Shopkeeper:** Iyeh, kbir wla sghir?
> **You:** Kbir, w atini jouj dyal l-chips.
> **Shopkeeper:** Hak. 15 dirham.
> **You:** Hak, shukran.
> **Shopkeeper:** Bslama!

**Translation:**
> **You:** Hello, do you have water?
> **Shopkeeper:** Yes, big or small?
> **You:** Big, and give me two bags of chips.
> **Shopkeeper:** Here. 15 dirhams.
> **You:** Here you go, thanks.
> **Shopkeeper:** Goodbye!

## Cultural Notes

### 🏪 Where to Bargain and Where NOT to
**Bargain:** souks, medina shops, street vendors, grand taxis, unlicensed guides.
**Don't bargain:** supermarkets (Marjane, Carrefour, BIM), pharmacies, restaurants, cafes, official taxis with meters, train tickets, bus tickets.

### 💰 The Art of Haggling
Bargaining in Morocco follows an unwritten ritual:
1. The vendor names a high price (often 2-3x the real value)
2. You act shocked ("Ghali bezzaf!")
3. You counter with about 40-50% of their ask
4. You go back and forth
5. You meet somewhere in the middle
6. If you can't agree, **walk away** — they'll often call you back

**Rule of thumb:** Aim to pay about 50-60% of the first asking price.

### 🤝 It's Social, Not Hostile
Bargaining is expected and enjoyed. Vendors aren't trying to rip you off — they're playing the game and expect you to play back. Smile, joke, be friendly. "Ghali 3lik a sahbi!" (Too expensive for me, my friend!) with a grin goes a long way.

### 🍵 Tea Means Nothing (in terms of obligation)
Vendors may offer you tea. It's genuine hospitality, but it does NOT obligate you to buy. You can drink the tea, chat, and still say "Ma bghitsh" and walk out. Don't feel guilty.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Bargaining at Fixed-Price Shops
Don't try to haggle at supermarkets, pharmacies, or chain stores. Prices are marked and fixed. You'll just embarrass yourself.

### ❌ Showing Too Much Excitement
If you say "Wow, 3jbni bezzaf hada!" (I LOVE this!) before asking the price, you've lost all bargaining power. Stay cool, act mildly interested.

### ❌ Starting Too High
If the vendor says 400, don't counter with 350. That's barely negotiating. Start around 40-50% of their price and work from there.

### ❌ Confusing "Safi" Contexts
"Safi" can mean "enough," "done," "okay," or "that's it" depending on context. "Safi, bslama" = I'm done, goodbye. "Safi, nakhod" = Alright, I'll take it. Listen for context.

### ❌ Forgetting to Count Your Change
Always count your change, especially in busy markets. It's not about distrust — it's about busy environments where mistakes happen.
