# Module 1: First Words — Greetings & Politeness

## Overview
Your first step into Moroccan Darija! In this module, you'll learn the essential greetings and polite expressions that Moroccans use every single day. These are the words you'll hear within your first minute in Morocco — and the ones that will instantly earn you smiles and respect from locals.

---

## Vocabulary (20 Words)

| # | Darija | Pronunciation | English | Notes |
|---|--------|--------------|---------|-------|
| 1 | **Salam** | sa-LAHM | Hello / Peace | Universal greeting, works everywhere |
| 2 | **Salam 3alaykom** | sa-LAHM ah-LAY-koom | Peace be upon you | More formal/respectful greeting |
| 3 | **Wa 3alaykom salam** | wa ah-LAY-koom sa-LAHM | And upon you peace | Reply to Salam 3alaykom |
| 4 | **Labas** | la-BASS | How are you? / I'm fine | Both a question AND an answer |
| 5 | **Labas 3lik** | la-BASS ah-LEEK | How are you? (to a male) | Adding 3lik makes it a direct question |
| 6 | **Labas 3lik** | la-BASS ah-LEEK | How are you? (to a female) | Same spelling, context matters |
| 7 | **Bikhir** | bee-KHEER | I'm well / Good | Common response to "how are you" |
| 8 | **Hamdullah** | ham-DOO-lah | Thanks to God | Said after "how are you" — always |
| 9 | **Shukran** | SHOOK-ran | Thank you | Used everywhere |
| 10 | **Bslama** | b-SLA-ma | Goodbye | Literally "with peace/safety" |
| 11 | **3afak** | ah-FAK | Please | Essential politeness word |
| 12 | **Smeh li** | SMEH lee | Excuse me / Sorry | To get attention or apologize |
| 13 | **La** | LA | No | Short and direct |
| 14 | **Iyeh / Ah** | ee-YEH / AH | Yes | Both are commonly used |
| 15 | **Mrhba** | MER-hba | Welcome | Said to greet someone arriving |
| 16 | **Allah y3awnk** | allah yah-AWNK | God help you | Said to someone working |
| 17 | **Tbark Allah** | t-BARK allah | God bless (compliment) | Said when admiring something |
| 18 | **Sbah lkhir** | SBAH l-KHEER | Good morning | Literally "morning of goodness" |
| 19 | **Msa lkhir** | MSA l-KHEER | Good evening | Literally "evening of goodness" |
| 20 | **Tsbah 3la khir** | ts-BAH ah-la KHEER | Good night | Said when parting at night |

### Understanding the Number System in Transliteration
- **3** = the Arabic letter "ع" (ain) — a deep throat sound. Think of tightening your throat and saying "ah"
- **7** = the Arabic letter "ح" (ha) — a breathy H from the throat
- **9** = the Arabic letter "ق" (qaf) — a deep K from the back of the throat
- **kh** = the Arabic letter "خ" — like the "ch" in Scottish "loch"
- **gh** = the Arabic letter "غ" — like a French "r" gargle

## Dialogue 1: Meeting Someone on the Street

> **You:** Salam!
> **Moroccan:** Salam! Labas 3lik?
> **You:** Labas, hamdullah. W nta, labas?
> **Moroccan:** Bikhir, hamdullah.
> **You:** Bslama!
> **Moroccan:** Bslama!

**Translation:**
> **You:** Hello!
> **Moroccan:** Hello! How are you?
> **You:** I'm fine, thanks to God. And you, are you fine?
> **Moroccan:** I'm well, thanks to God.
> **You:** Goodbye!
> **Moroccan:** Goodbye!

---

## Dialogue 2: Entering a Shop

> **You:** Salam 3alaykom!
> **Shopkeeper:** Wa 3alaykom salam! Mrhba, mrhba!
> **You:** Labas?
> **Shopkeeper:** Labas, hamdullah. Tfddal! (Come in / Go ahead)
> **You:** Shukran!

**Translation:**
> **You:** Peace be upon you!
> **Shopkeeper:** And upon you peace! Welcome, welcome!
> **You:** How are you?
> **Shopkeeper:** Fine, thanks to God. Come right in!
> **You:** Thank you!

---

## Dialogue 3: Morning at the Hotel

> **You:** Sbah lkhir!
> **Receptionist:** Sbah lkhir! Labas, kol shi bikhir?
> **You:** Iyeh, kol shi bikhir, hamdullah.
> **Receptionist:** Mzyan! (Good!)

**Translation:**
> **You:** Good morning!
> **Receptionist:** Good morning! Are you well, everything okay?
> **You:** Yes, everything is fine, thanks to God.
> **Receptionist:** Great!

---

## Dialogue 4: Saying Goodbye to a Friend

> **You:** Yallah, ana ghadi nmshi. Bslama!
> **Friend:** Bslama! Tsbah 3la khir!
> **You:** W nta tsbah 3la khir!

**Translation:**
> **You:** Alright, I'm going to go. Goodbye!
> **Friend:** Goodbye! Good night!
> **You:** And you, good night!

## Cultural Notes

### 1. Always Say Hamdullah
When someone asks how you are, Moroccans ALWAYS include "hamdullah" (thanks to God) in their response, even if they're having a terrible day. It's cultural — you thank God regardless. Skipping it sounds strange.

### 2. Greetings Are Never Rushed
In Morocco, greetings are a ritual. You don't just say "hi" and move on. Expect a back-and-forth exchange: "How are you? How's the family? How's work? How's your health?" Even with a stranger selling you bread. This is not small talk — it's respect.

### 3. Labas Works Both Ways
"Labas" is magical in Darija — it's simultaneously a question ("Are you okay?") and an answer ("I'm okay"). Context and tone do the work. Rising intonation = question. Flat = answer.

### 4. Physical Greetings
Between men: handshake, often followed by placing your right hand on your heart. Close friends may kiss on both cheeks (or one cheek, touching cheek to cheek).
Between women: cheek kisses (usually two).
Between men and women: it depends on the individuals and their level of religiosity. Follow their lead. A hand on heart is always safe.

### 5. Right Hand Rule
Always greet, eat, and give/receive with your right hand. The left hand is considered unclean in Moroccan culture.

2. A: Sbah _______!
   B: Sbah lkhir! Labas?

   B: La shukran 3la wajib! (No thanks needed — it's my duty!)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Forgetting Hamdullah
**Wrong:** "Labas?" → "Labas."
**Right:** "Labas?" → "Labas, hamdullah."
Always add hamdullah. It's not optional — it's cultural DNA.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Using MSA Greetings
**Wrong:** "Marhaba" (this is Modern Standard Arabic, not Darija)
**Right:** "Salam" or "Labas"
Moroccans will understand MSA but it sounds textbook-stiff. Use Darija to connect.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Rushing Past Greetings
**Wrong:** Walking into a shop and immediately asking for what you want.
**Right:** "Salam! Labas?" → wait for response → THEN state your business.
Skipping greetings is considered rude. Always greet first.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Saying "Shukran" with an "oo" Sound
**Wrong:** "SHOO-kran"
**Right:** "SHOOK-ran" (the U is short, like in "book")

### ❌ Mistake 5: Mixing Up Goodbye Times
**Wrong:** Saying "Tsbah 3la khir" at noon.
**Right:** "Tsbah 3la khir" is only for nighttime partings. During the day, just use "Bslama."
