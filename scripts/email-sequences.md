## Email 2: Free Lesson Teaser

Subject: Your first free Darija lesson: greetings that sound natural

Preview text: Learn what Moroccans actually say when they meet.

Hi {{first_name}},

Today I want to give you a small free lesson you can use immediately.

Many learners begin with textbook Arabic, then arrive in Morocco and realize people speak differently in daily life.

For example, instead of a formal phrase, you will often hear:

Salam, labas?

This means:

Hello, how are you? / Is everything okay?

A natural answer is:

Labas, hamdullah. Nta?

If you are speaking to a woman:

Labas, hamdullah. Nti?

Meaning:

Good, thank God. And you?

Notice two useful words:

Nta = you, masculine.  
Nti = you, feminine.

Practice this mini dialogue:

Person A: Salam, labas?  
Person B: Labas, hamdullah. Nta?  
Person A: Bikhir, shukran.

Translation:

Person A: Hello, how are you?  
Person B: Good, thank God. And you?  
Person A: Good, thank you.

Pronunciation tip:

Do not make Darija too slow or too formal. Listen for the rhythm. Moroccans often speak quickly, but the phrases are short. Practice slowly first, then say the full exchange naturally.

Your next step:

Record yourself saying the dialogue once slowly and once naturally. Then listen back. You will immediately notice which words need more practice.

Tomorrow, try using one phrase with a Moroccan friend, family member, teacher, shop owner, or online language partner.

Bslama,  
Elias

Subject: "I finally understood what my Moroccan family was saying"

Preview text: Why small Darija wins matter.

Hi {{first_name}},

One of the most common messages I hear from learners is this:

"I do not need to sound perfect. I just want to understand and be part of the conversation."

That is exactly why Belditalk exists.

Some students learn Darija because they married into a Moroccan family. Some are preparing for a trip. Some live in Morocco and want daily life to feel easier. Others simply love Moroccan culture and want to connect more deeply.

The first breakthrough is usually small:

- Understanding when someone says `daba`, meaning now.
- Knowing that `safi` can mean okay, enough, done, or agreed.
- Ordering at a cafe without switching immediately to French or English.
- Saying `tbark Allah` at the right moment when admiring something.
- Recognizing `bzzaf`, meaning a lot or very.

These moments matter because language is not only vocabulary. It is access.

When you understand Darija, you understand more jokes, more warmth, more family conversations, more street interactions, and more of Morocco itself.

Here is a simple learner exercise for today:

Learn these three high-frequency words:

Daba = now.  
Safi = okay / enough / done.  
Bzzaf = a lot / very.

Example sentences:

Daba? - Now?  
Safi, shukran. - Okay, thank you.  
Zwina bzzaf. - Very beautiful.

Your next step:

Choose one of these words and use it in a sentence today. It can be as simple as:

Safi, I understand.

That is how progress starts.
